What We Do

The Art of a
Clean Chimney

A dirty chimney is one of the leading causes of house fires in Canada. Creosote — the tar-like byproduct of burning wood — accumulates inside your flue and can ignite without warning. Our thorough sweep removes creosote, blockages, debris, and animal nests from your entire chimney system.

  • Complete flue cleaning with professional rotary brushing systems
  • Creosote removal at all three stages of buildup
  • Firebox, smoke chamber, and damper inspection included
  • Cap and crown condition check
  • Before and after documentation on request
  • Clean work — drop cloths used, full cleanup on exit
What is Creosote?

The Hidden Fire Risk
in Every Chimney

Creosote forms when smoke cools inside the flue and the unburned gases condense on the flue walls. It comes in three stages:

Stage 1 — Flaky
Light, dusty — easily brushed out during a standard sweep.
Stage 2 — Tar-Like
Hard, crunchy — requires professional tools and techniques.
Stage 3 — Glazed ⚠
Dense, shiny — extremely flammable. Requires specialized chemical treatment. Do not use your fireplace if stage 3 is present.

What is a WETT Inspection?

Canada's Standard
for Solid Fuel Systems

WETT (Wood Energy Technology Transfer) is the national certification body for wood burning system professionals in Canada. A WETT inspection is the recognized standard for assessing the safety and compliance of fireplaces, wood stoves, inserts, and chimneys.

Insurance companies, mortgage lenders, and real estate transactions frequently require a WETT certificate before coverage or sale can be completed.

  • Level 1 — Visual inspection of accessible areas
  • Level 2 — Full inspection including video scan of flue interior
  • Level 3 — Invasive inspection for serious concerns
  • WETT certificate issued upon successful completion
  • Accepted by all major Canadian insurance carriers
  • Required for most BC real estate transactions involving solid fuel appliances
